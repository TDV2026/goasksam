A custom louvered decklid and side panels were added in 2006, and the hood was fitted with a central fuel filler a few years later. The chassis has been updated with an Elephant Racing QuickChange rear spring plate kit and a Weltmeister 19mm front sway bar. Power comes from a 1,582cc flat-four with dual Weber 40 IDF carburetors, and it is linked to a Type 741 four-speed manual transaxle. Inside, aluminum Speedster-style seats are complemented by square-weave carpets and a Nardi steering wheel. The car was acquired by the current owner in 2009, and service performed in 2021 included refreshing the fuel system, powder-coating the wheels, and replacing the tires. An extensive collection of parts, literature, correspondence, and service records will accompany the car. This custom 356B is now offered on dealer consignment with a clean Michigan title listing it as a 1961 model. Modified 1960 Porsche 356B Sunroof Coupe The car was modified and refurbished in several stages over the course of more than 40 years with its prior owner. The car was stripped to bare metal in 2011, and the bumper mounts were filled and a central fuel filler was added to the hood. The factory sliding sunroof was retained. The car was then repainted in its factory shade of Silver Metallic (6006), per the selling dealer. Modified 1960 Porsche 356B Sunroof Coupe The rear bodywork was customized in 2006 at Specialty Metal Fabrication of Monterey, California, with an enlarged decklid that incorporates an air scoop, ventilation louvers, and a license plate light assembly with an integrated center stop light. Louvered panels were installed in place of the quarter windows, and teardrop taillights were mounted vertically above the reflectors. Modified 1960 Porsche 356B Sunroof Coupe Color-keyed 15” wide-five wheels are wrapped in Hoosier Speedster tires that were mounted in 2021. The suspension has been lowered by 1”, and Elephant Racing QuickChange rear spring plates with PolyBronze bearings have been installed along with a Weltmeister 19mm front sway bar. Braking is handled by four-wheel drums. Modified 1960 Porsche 356B Sunroof Coupe Aluminum Speedster-style seats are mounted to sliding rails and feature black and gray upholstery with ring-grommet ventilation and lap harnesses. Sound insulation material was added to the cabin under black square-weave carpets. Modified 1960 Porsche 356B Sunroof Coupe The wood-rimmed Nardi steering wheel sits ahead of green-letter VDO instrumentation consisting of a 120-mph speedometer, a tachometer with a 4,500-rpm redline, and a combination gauge for oil temperature and fuel level. A Stewart-Warner ammeter and a VDO oil pressure gauge have also been added. The five-digit odometer shows 98k miles, approximately 600 of which have been added under current ownership.
